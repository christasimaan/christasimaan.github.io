Senior Design

For my senior design project, I worked with four others to use NLP models to combat the mental health crises. We analyzed Reddit data from global universities’ subreddit forums, focused on keywords that pertained to mental health and loneliness. We leveraged this data to examine the relationship between mental health and the respective university to analyze changes in sentiment of posts pertaining to mental wellness before, during, and after the Covid-19 pandemic. Children's Hospital of Philadelphia Refrigerator Workflow Redesign

There were issues with the workflow and organization of human milk and formula in refrigerators and freezers at Children's Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP). These problems included, but are not limited to: spills in the refrigerators and when pouring freshly pumped milk into storage containers, space constraints for bottles in the refrigerator, labeling issues, and workflow and process variability across CHOP. These issues impacted the workload of the nurses, which in turn has the potential to limit the quality of patient care. The purpose of our project was to explore root causes of these issues and identify potential solutions. After tons of user research, direct observations, and clinical research, we landed on a few different prototypes. First, we were inspired by the efficiency and simplicity of egg trays in refrigerators, we proposed a fridge storage tray solution to facilitate human milk and formula storage in hospital settings, while increasing space available and decreasing the mental workload for nurses at CHOP. We mocked-up and then 3D printed the fridge storage tray below. The prototype mimics egg trays’ segmented layout, offering modular compartments that facilitate organized storage. Each compartment is designed for easy access and a first-in, first-out (FIFO) arrangement, optimizing space and ensuring the use of milk before expiration. This solution optimizes the use of space and makes human milk storage much more compact than before. We recognized that the initial egg tray-inspired prototype still lacked space efficiency and adaptability with existing organizational processes. More specifically, the overwhelming consensus from nurses we spoke with and sent our questionnaire to was that space constraints were a major problem, and we wanted to tackle this more directly with our solution. To address these shortcomings, we looked towards lab storage designs for inspiration, leading to a modular approach that integrates seamlessly with nurses’ existing workflows. Our new prototype allows milk bottles to be stackable, significantly reducing the space constraint, and also allows bottles to be accessed easily. Our redesign was mainly focused around space efficiency, modularity, ease of use, and workflow simplifications. For space efficiency, the new prototype prioritizes a compact design to maximize storage capacity in existing refrigeration units. It is the height of the shelves in the chop fridges which are about 7-8 rack units, and easily is able to be placed on a rack within the fridges. It is very modular, as there are inserts to be adaptable based on varying storage needs. It is easy to use as we ensure trays are user-friendly, easily replaceable, and compatible with the current practices within CHOP fridges. Last, but not least the modular design aligns with nurses’ routines, allowing for quicker and error-free access to milk containers. Additionally, due to their size, nurses will be able to use more racks as needed, they are easy to replace if they break, and can have dividers put into sections off the tray. This could be by date pumped, date of expiration, patient, etc. This redesigned prototype represents a significant improvement, harmonizing with the dynamic environment of human milk and formula storage and simplifying management for the nursing staff.

FlavorFinder

FlavorFinder is designed to help alleviate the pain point of having certain ingredients and not knowing what to do with them either before they go bad as well as inspiring creativity in the kitchen. This project is designed to create and make an interactive interface that helps users select a recipe based on desired ingredients, ingredient quantities, nutritional value, prep time, cook time, recipe reviews, and so much more! This was done using SQL, JavaScript, Python, and more.
Our application has many different forms of functionality. To start, the website opens with a log-in/sign-up page that allows new users to sign-up and existing users to log-in to their account. The log-in/sign-up formal is followed by a home/recipes page that consists of a featured recipe and a top recipes table. Then, there is a search page where users can type in a name or description of a recipe they are looking for and use sliders to check on the time, content, recipe yield, ingredients, etc. The website then uses these search parameters to query the dataset and display the query result of the recipes that meet all the inputted preferences. Each individual recipe links to a page that displays all information about that recipe, including the ingredients list, prep time, cook time, instructions, nutrition facts, reviews, and more. This is followed by similar logic, but into a reviews page. The reviews page has a search bar as well to look for certain authors, reviews, or ratings. Lastly, there is also a Likes page which shows the recipes that a user has liked and saves them for the user to have easy access to.
We found the overarching dataset we used here on Kaggle. The dataset had two .csv files in it, one being Recipes which is found here and the other Reviews which are found here. The Recipes’ table is 716.4 MB. There were 522,569 rows before cleaning and after cleaning there were 28,013 rows and 30 columns. The Reviews’ table is 504.9 MB. There were 1,401,982 rows before cleaning and after cleaning there were 1,401,768 rows and 8 columns.

Climate Change Predictor

We used an Earth Surface Temperature dataset, as well as two additional .csv files on both greenhouse gases and natural disaster rates in order to analyze information about the varying surface temperatures on Earth (and many of the possible consequences of such actions). This was done in a Jupyter Notebook using pandas, python, and machine learning techniques, SARIMA.
This project is rather interesting because climate change has been more and more of an increasing issue. We dove into how it has and will continue to affect countries, major cities, and states. The overall structure of our process was as follows: Data Wrangling of the State, City, and Country tables Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Visualization of the Average Temperatures using machine learning to make temperature predictions connection to natural disasters. We use different techniques and figures to represent the interconnection and correlations between these surface temperatures with various possible atrocities so that we could see for ourselves the trends. This is a topic that often has misinformation and we have seen through working with the data that there are many exceptions from the overall trends that can be cherry picked in order to demonstrate certain narratives.
As a synopsis, we did data analysis pulling in four different .csv with around 700,000 rows of information in each. Then, we analyzed climate change over the years and calculated the predicted temperatures using regression in 2050 for different states, countries, and major cities. We also analyzed how climate change has an effect on natural disasters.
Our main challenge with the project above was that since data and temperatures fluctuate depending on where one is located in the world as the seasons are different, we were first using AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) and then did not get predictions which seemed correct. So, we used Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average (SARIMA), which would give us seasonal information and data and then successfully were able to predict the 2050 temperatures based on our data sets.
The timeline in which we predict most of our models to span is until 2050. We found that any further predictions would not reflect the state of the world accurately. We hope you enjoy the cool, groundbreaking results from this dataset and can potentially re-observe them in 2050 to see if the temperature predictions held true.

Latchless

Ideated, came-up with a product, completed assembly drawings, proof of concepts, schematic design, and more for my product, Latchless! Latchless is a portable contraption that lets you open and close your doors with ease. This new and improved doorstop allows for one to fully close their door all while making it latchless by successfully pushing the latch all the way back. By “removing” the latch, the door essentially becomes a swinging door and is thus able to open with just a nudge which is perfect for when you have a bunch of things to carry in. By having this contraption reside on your keys it is rather portable and it makes for easy access and trips inside when doing laundry, grocery shopping, moving-in, etc. It is very user-friendly and can be put on in seconds.
The prototype is an innovative and versatile product that combines four distinct components to create a single solution for a common problem. We utilized laser cut acrylic, stretchy elastic wrist band, key rings, and an adjustable strap. The laser cut acrylic is ⅛’ in order to have the door still be able to close, but strong enough to keep the latch back. The stretchy elastic wrist band allows for it to also be universal and stretch to size based on the width of the door handle from the latch. The key ring, made of sturdy metal, allows for connecting the parts together. The adjustable strap serves an essential purpose by allowing for the band to be adjustable and thus universal for every door.
This prototype has several advantages, including its versatility and durability, which make it an ideal solution for both personal and professional use. Its unique design and ease of use make it an attractive option for anyone looking for a practical and effective way to prevent doors from closing or to keep keys organized. Overall, this innovative prototype is a promising solution that has the potential to solve a common problem in a convenient and effective way.
In our product design fair, this product was awarded $448,672 due to the cost of production and the design fair tokens received (each attendee of the design fair was given three tokens simulating a product vote/buy) and received second place out of 43 different products at the fair.
